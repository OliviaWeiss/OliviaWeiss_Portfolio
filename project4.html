Book Cover Design

Design Statement

The book Heads-Up Baseball is aimed at athletes and coaches and covers mental toughness, confidence and self belief, emotional control, and process oriented thinking. These themes are represented with a blueprint of a baseball diamond, signifying strategic planning, navigating the game, control, and the connection between physical and mental, using metaphors, analogy, and allusion. The blueprint of a baseball diamond can serve as a metaphor for the mental strategy part of the game, as the book provides a mental blueprint to help success on the field. The blueprint also serves as an analogy for how mental preparation in baseball is crucial just like with a traditional house blueprint. Without a thought out plan things could crash and fall. The final trope used was allusion and that was brought again through the blueprint design alluding to the concept of building success. This makes an association of how athletes build their skills and mindsets over time, just like how buildings take shape over time from their blueprints.

Key Features

  • Metaphorical Visualization: The blueprint of a baseball diamond represents mental preparation, discipline, and strategic thinking.
  • Blueprint Aesthetic: Technical drawing style with grids, measurements, and linework symbolizing structure and planning.
  • Color Psychology: Calming shades of blue and white evoke clarity, focus, and composure under pressure.
  • Typography System: Geometric, architectural typefaces reinforce the precision and structure of blueprints.

Process Work

The process began with sketches to explore composition and concept, then moved into digital exploration to test interaction, layout, and final visual language.

Sketches

Rapid ideation and thumbnail sketches to capture composition, hierarchy, and visual metaphor before committing to digital drafts.

Digital Exploration

Digital mockups and exploratory compositions—testing type systems, layout grids, and color relationships for the cover design.
